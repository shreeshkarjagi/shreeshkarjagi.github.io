Magnetoelastic Generator(MEG)-Based Respiration Strain Sensor

  • Category: Self Powered Wearable Bioelectronics
  • Class: Bioengineering laboratory
  • Link: Detailed Report

Project detail

In our lab sections, our team fabricated an MEG-based respiration strain sensor. We fabricated this device using PDMS, 25 μm magnetic nanoparticles, copper wire, aluminum foil, physiotherapy bands, and tape. This device was used to collect normal breathing and hard exhale data by placing the device on the diaphragm and the chest. We measured the current of normal breathing, rapid breathing, and forced expiration for two individuals, and aim to use this data to measure lung tidal volume and forced vital capacity. With this device, we hope to establish a method of timely and correct diagnosis of chronic respiratory diseases and engage in proper symptom management in order to decrease symptoms, reduce the frequency and severity of exacerbations, and improve health status.
