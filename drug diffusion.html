Mathematical models for drug diffusion through different compartments

  • Category: Numerical Methods and Modeling
  • Class: Engineering Principles of Drug Delivery
  • Link: Detailed Paper

Project detail

This paper is an attempt to develop mathematical models to better understand drug distribution in the human body via oral intake. Fick's principle and the law of mass action were used to develop a model based on the diffusion process. The rate constants were selected based on the drug efficacy at different interfaces. The ordinary differential equations (ODEs) describing the rate of change of concentration in compartments, such as stomach, lumen, arterial blood, tissue medium, and venous blood were solved using MATLAB. This was used to produce graphs depicting the change of drug concentration over time. Numerical parameters were used to calculate the drug concentration in the different compartments.
